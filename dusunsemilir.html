Bayou Country

Bayou Country is a themed area inspired by the enchanting atmosphere of Louisiana’s riverfront and swamp life, particularly the charm of the American South. It captures the spirit of Creole and Cajun culture with lively jazz music, the aroma of Southern cuisine, and the magical ambiance of glowing lanterns and moss-draped trees. The architecture throughout Bayou Country reflects French colonial design, with ornate wrought-iron balconies, vibrant colors, and festive Mardi Gras decorations. The area celebrates warmth, music, storytelling, and the rich heritage brought to life by tales like The Princess and the Frog.

1. Tiana’s Bayou Adventure

This upcoming log-flume ride follows Princess Tiana after The Princess and the Frog movie. She journeys through the magical bayou to find a special ingredient for her Mardi Gras party. Guests will enjoy lively animatronics, glowing swamp scenes, and a thrilling final drop — all set to New Orleans-inspired music.

2. Mark Twain Riverboat

A peaceful steam-powered riverboat ride along the Rivers of America. Guests can relax and view bayou-style landscapes, wildlife, and Southern scenery. Though located near Frontierland, it complements the Bayou Country theme with slow-paced storytelling and historical charm.

3. Character Meet & Greet with Princess Tiana

Guests can meet Princess Tiana in a beautiful Southern-inspired setting, take photos, and get autographs. She sometimes appears with Prince Naveen, especially during seasonal events like Mardi Gras, offering a personal touch to the bayou experience.
